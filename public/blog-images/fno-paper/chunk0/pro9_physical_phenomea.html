PDEs Describe Nearly Every Physical Phenomenon

From fluid flow to quantum mechanics — and solving them is computationally expensive

▶
Fluid Dynamics
Navier-Stokes
Describes the motion of fluids — from ocean currents to airflow around aircraft wings. Notoriously difficult to solve; a $1M prize awaits anyone who proves solutions always exist.
▶
Heat Transfer
Heat Equation
Models how temperature distributes over time. Used in everything from cooling electronics to predicting Earth's climate. One of the oldest and most studied PDEs.
▶
Electromagnetics
Maxwell's Equations
Governs light, radio waves, and all electromagnetic phenomena. Foundation of modern communication, from WiFi to satellite links. Unified electricity and magnetism.
▶
Weather & Climate
Primitive Equations
Atmospheric dynamics combining fluid flow, thermodynamics, and rotation. Weather forecasts require solving these on massive supercomputers — FNO is changing this.
▶
Quantum Mechanics
Schrödinger Equation
Describes how quantum states evolve. Essential for understanding atoms, molecules, and semiconductors. The basis for all modern electronics and quantum computing.
▶
Structural Mechanics
Elasticity Equations
Models deformation of solid materials under stress. Critical for designing bridges, buildings, aircraft, and any structure that must withstand forces without breaking.
Traditional solvers can take hours or days for a single simulation
